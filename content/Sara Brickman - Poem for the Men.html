Poem for the Men Who Write Poems About Women's Stories, And Make Themselves Look Glorious in the Telling

Sara Brickman

You think you know what its like

to be a galaxy that grew around a knife

but you don’t.

You think you know how the ozone feels

        numb    and a million miles above the earth

full of holes that we put there

but you always forget

                she has the best view of the stars.

The things you don’t know

could fill several mass graves with wedding rings

the dirt you shovel into our mouths unknowingly

when we tell our ugly and are brushed aside

you: brave                   we: shrill

         you: honest                                we

a spill no one wants to mop up.

You say I am a diamond, to shine so much

after being pulled from the darkest mines,

but I’m just a rock that’s been under

                                    pressure a long time.

        OR

                           you look at me like I'm coal

that needs cleaning

but there is no cleaning coal                             idiot.

You think you have answers

                                                for me?        Advice

I might find useful

on how to love my body                    love myself

apologies for men

            you will make from a stage but don't

                        quite know how to phrase

to the wandering hands at the bar

the streetcorner hyenas with choke chain voices

                        Do you ever get tired of pinning

            butterflies to cork?

                                                Of rummaging

through baskets of skulls

to measure the width of our noses,

                                    just trying to boil us down

                  to something         “scientific?”

            Did you know

when salmon return

to the place of their birth to spawn

their bodies are destroyed on the journey

and they make love in the water

                                                   already dying?

Good intentions can make us do crazy things.

Fuck with our skin coming off                        Speak

before we look to see

                        if somebody else is already speaking

Build a bomb shelter              in a nuclear wasteland

        It's truet              that in the wild

some animals will help lesser animals stay alive

in forest fires                 avalanches

like they know their continued survival         relies

on having something

                                   lower

            in the food chain to devour

            when they get out of the snowdrift

it’s true

                       solidarity is a thing that can exist

can be necessary, even.

But no bear tries to give the salmon

tips on how to swim

when its body is a hundred times heavier

                                               than lightning quick

when all instructions have to pass through the bear’s

pointed teeth

just to ripple in the salmon’s pink    flesh    back

Do you really want to be the one

telling me about resilience

                        through that dripping jaw

when every drop of water I swim in

                        has to pass through your mouth first?

I swim upstream every day

                                  just to get to somewhere

                                  that hasn’t lived in your blood

As if my body wasn’t decomposing in the water

            and I didn’t love         through it

                                                                   anyway.
